Family Sponsorship Processing Criteria

  1. VisaToCanada (VTC) provides JM Immigration (JMI) with potential clients’ file for assessment. (assess eligibility as a sponsor and assess if the person being sponsored is eligible for permanent residence)

  2. JMI assesses profile and advises VTC if client meets eligibility criteria.
    a) If yes, VTC advises JMI if client ready to begin processing.
    b) If No, JMI provides VTC the reasons.

  3. Client signs retainer” and “use of representative form”, returns both to JMI.

  4. Client pays the first milestone payment to start processing, 50% of fees. Client makes a wire transfer.

  5. Client pays the first milestone payment to start processing, 50% of fees. Client makes a wire transfer.

  6. JMI provides client with documentation checklist.

  7. Client provide JMI with passport copy and all documents as per the checklist.

  8. JMI provides client with all applicable forms for completion.

  9. JMI reviews all forms for completeness and correctness.

  10. JMI validates forms that require validation and send all forms to client for signature.

  11. JMI advices client to pay government fees.

  12. JMI submits application package to IRCC.

  13. IRCC will send
    1) application number
    2) acknowledgement of receipt

  14. IRCC will send request to each family member to complete medical exam, and give biometrics. JMI will advise client accordingly.

  15. Once IRCC has approved family members’ permanent residence application and to finish processing their application, your spouse, partner and child, and their dependents who are immigrating to Canada with them (if applicable), JMI request client to provide passports to send to IRCC:
    • Their passports, or photocopies of their passports (we’ll let you know which one they should send)
    • 2 photos
    • Proof of payment for the right of permanent residence fee, if you haven’t sent it to us already.

  16. IRCC upon completion of processing their application will send:
    • A document confirming, they have been approved to become Canadian permanent residents. This is document is called the Confirmation of Permanent Residence.
    • A permanent resident visa, if required
    o If the persons you’re sponsoring are from countries whose citizens need a visa to travel to Canada, we’ll give them a permanent resident visa in their passport. We’ll do this even if they’re already in Canada.

  17. Once your family members receive these documents, they can travel to Canada.

  18. When the sponsored family members arrive at the port of entry to Canada, they must show the border services officer their:
    • Confirmation of Permanent Residence
    o This is the document we sent when we approved their application
    • Valid passport or travel document
    • Permanent resident visa (if we issued one), which must be valid
    • Quebec Selection Certificate, if they’re going to live in Quebec
    • Any other document we issued to them and told you to bring

  19. The border officer will:
    • Check that their permanent resident visa and confirmation of permanent residence have not expired.
    • Ask the family member to confirm information that they gave in their application.

Sample Checklist – Spousal Sponsorship
  • Be a Canadian citizen or permanent resident of Canada
  • Be 18 or older
  • Be prepared to financially support your spouse (and any dependents)
  • have not failed to comply with previous sponsorship agreements
  • have not failed to pay court ordered child support or alimony
  • have not required financial assistance for anything other than disability
  • have not been convicted of or threatened violent or sexual crimes or crimes against family members.
  • have not been sponsored to come to Canada within the last five years
  • have not been in prison
  • have not declared bankruptcy and not be released
